Course Overview

About the Course

ASA City is a comprehensive urban development project created as part of the Advanced City Development course. The project integrates principles of urban planning, architectural design, transportation engineering, sustainability, and smart city technologies into a unified city model.

The core objective of ASA City is to demonstrate how theoretical concepts can be translated into practical and functional urban environments. we worked through studio sessions, case studies, and collaborative design tasks to transform conceptual planning ideas into a detailed city layout. A key emphasis of the project is on the use of data structures and algorithms as planning tools. The team explored how road networks operate like weighted graphs, zoning mimics hierarchical tree structures, and urban services depend on efficient searching and optimization. Through this approach, ASA City shows that modern city planning is fundamentally a data-driven process. ASA City integrates multimodal transport, pedestrian-friendly public spaces, and sustainable land use. Smart technologies are embedded into the model to support connectivity, resource efficiency, safety, and responsive governance. The project underlines how algorithmic thinking improves decision-making in city design: optimizing routes, predicting risks, allocating services, and reducing costs. Overall, ASA City represents a complete city concept that combines planning theory, teamwork, and real-world application. It illustrates how urban development can be enhanced through strategic use of analytics, structured data, and intelligent systems, resulting in a modern, resilient, and livable city.

What You Will Learn

  • Fundamentals of urban planning and city structure
  • Designing road networks, blocks, and land-use zoning
  • Integrating public transport and pedestrian-friendly spaces
  • Applying sustainability principles in city development
  • Using technology to create smart and connected cities
  • Working in teams to plan and present a complete city concept
